Introduction

Hydropower (hydroelectric) generation uses flowing water to produce electricity. It's a renewable source that converts the potential and kinetic energy of water into mechanical energy with turbines and then into electrical energy with generators.

Simple Working Diagram

How it works (short)

Water stored at height (in reservoir) flows through a penstock to a turbine; the moving water spins the turbine which turns a generator to make electricity. Power depends on the water flow rate (Q) and head (H).

Basic formula (use in practical)

Electrical Power (approx): P = ρ * g * Q * H * η

Where: ρ = water density (~1000 kg/m³), g = 9.81 m/s², Q = flow (m³/s), H = head (m), η = efficiency (0–1).

Mini example

If Q = 5 m³/s, H = 20 m, efficiency = 0.85 then

P = 1000 * 9.81 * 5 * 20 * 0.85 ≈ 834, 000 W ≈ 834 kW

Key Components

  • Reservoir / Dam — stores water and provides head.
  • Intake & Penstock — channels water to turbines.
  • Turbine — converts water energy to mechanical energy.
  • Generator — converts mechanical energy to electrical energy.
  • Control systems — gates, governors, and transformers.

Advantages

  • Renewable, low operating cost, fast dispatch.

Limitations

  • Environmental impact, site-specific, high initial cost.
